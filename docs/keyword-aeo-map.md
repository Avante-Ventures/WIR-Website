## 1. TL;DR Strategy

- **You are demand-capped, not ranking-capped.** In 90 days the site pulled ~610 impressions, but the *only* head term with real volume ("wir" / "observabilidade de agentes de ia") is **off-ICP** — developers shopping for LLM-monitoring tools (Langfuse crowd), not insurers. Chasing more of that volume grows vanity traffic, not pipeline. **Do not optimize for the dev-observability cluster.**
- **Bet on long-tail PT-BR + question queries.** Real insurance-ICP demand exists but is thin and specific: *automação de cotação*, *open insurance*, *fraude em sinistros*, *dados não estruturados*, *STP*. These already surface at positions 3-10 with almost no authority — the realistic win zone for a 6-week-old domain.
- **AEO is your highest-leverage channel.** Niche B2B insurance questions ("can I share my insurance history between companies in Brazil?", "how is open insurance being implemented in Brazil?") are *already* appearing in GSC at pos 5-10. These are exactly what gets cited in ChatGPT/Perplexity/Google AI answers. Structure content as direct answers (see §4).
- **Consolidate, don't expand the off-ICP page.** `observabilidade-de-agentes` is the #2 page but feeds the wrong audience. Re-frame toward *agent observability for insurers / auditability of AI decisions in underwriting* — or treat it as a loss-leader and stop linking it prominently.
- **Honest head-term reality:** PT-BR insurance-AI head terms are genuinely low-volume today. Win the long-tail now; head terms become viable only after authority + category awareness grow over 6-12 months.

## 6. Honest Caveats

- **No keyword-volume tool used** (no Ahrefs/Semrush/Keyword Planner this session). "Alto/Medio/Bajo" = *ICP-fit and rankability*, NOT verified search volume. Validate P1 in Google Keyword Planner before committing budget — expect genuinely low PT-BR volume (tens to low-hundreds/mo), which is *why* the long-tail + AEO strategy is correct.
- **GSC sample is tiny (~610 impr/90d)** — positions on 1-3-impression queries are noise. Weight higher-impression signals far more: observabilidade 66, wir 74, STP 23, MGAs 34, o-underwriter 24, open-insurance-en 18, roteamento-en 14.
- **"wir" (74 impr) is ambiguous** (German "we") — don't read as brand demand.
- **EN often out-ranks PT** on the same topic (thinner competition) — but PT-BR remains the ICP priority; EN is the easier AEO-citation win. Run both, lead PT for pipeline.
- **AEO is invisible in GSC** — citations in ChatGPT/Perplexity/AI Overviews won't show as clicks. Pair with referrer tracking + manual prompt-testing in the assistants.
